# PromptLab v2: Adversarial Co-Training for AI-Text Detectability Reduction
## Final Report — STAT 4830, University of Pennsylvania

---

## Abstract

PromptLab v2 is a pipeline for reducing the detectability of AI-generated essays under a fixed classifier. The system combines evolutionary prompt search (deslop), a multi-round co-training loop that accumulates contrastive essay pairs, and a T5-base model fine-tuned with LoRA trained to perform essay-to-essay rewriting in a single forward pass. The final model achieves a mean slop score of **0.0998** on 20 held-out validation pairs spanning 77 unique topics — a 74% reduction from the 0.391 prompt-rewriting baseline. A learning rate sweep and LoRA rank ablation confirm the optimal configuration: LR=1e-4, r=16, weight_decay=0.01, with early stopping on the task metric rather than cross-entropy loss.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

AI-generated text is detectable by classifiers that exploit stylistic signatures: hedging language, uniform sentence structure, excessive transitional phrases, and the characteristic cadence of instruction-tuned models. Reducing detectability requires discovering specific prompt strategies that suppress these patterns — a problem that is difficult to solve by manual inspection and requires systematic search.

PromptLab v2 frames this as an optimization problem over the space of essay-generating prompts, with a fixed classifier (editlens/roberta-large, "SlopDetector") providing the reward signal. The goal is to then distill the results of this search into a lightweight rewriter model that can produce low-slop essays without running expensive evolutionary search at inference time.

---

## 2. System Architecture

The pipeline consists of two main components.

### 2.1 deslop Optimizer

The evolutionary optimizer maintains a population of prompt candidates and applies mutation and crossover operations — guided by a mutator LLM (llama-3.1-8b-instant) — to produce variants, then scores the resulting essays and keeps the best. The scoring function is not simply the detector output; a drift penalty prevents the optimizer from gaming the detector with incoherent text:

```
optimization_slop = detector_slop
                  + α·(1 − cosine_similarity)
                  + β·(1 − ROUGE-L)
                  + γ·BERTScore_penalty
```

Critically, the optimizer uses **few-shot injection**: as it accumulates successful low-scoring essays across rounds, it prepends them as examples to future generation calls, so the search gets progressively better at finding the stylistic patterns the detector penalizes. Essays are generated via llama-3.3-70b-versatile through the Groq API.

Over hundreds of rounds on 77 unique topics, this process produced 372 cotrain pairs. The best single optimizer score achieved was 0.011 (social media & mental health topic).

### 2.2 T5 + LoRA Rewriter

A T5-base model fine-tuned with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) performs essay-to-essay rewriting in a single forward pass at inference time. The key architectural insight is **direct supervision alignment**: rather than mapping prompts to prompts (two steps from the detector signal), the rewriter maps high-slop essays directly to low-slop essays, with the training target being the actual essay the optimizer produced when it achieved its best score.

Training configuration:
- **AdamW** with decoupled weight decay (weight_decay=0.01)
- **Cosine learning rate decay** from LR=1e-4 with 50 warmup steps
- **Early stopping on slop_mean** — not eval_loss, which diverges from the task metric after step 50
- **Deterministic beam search** (num_beams=4, do_sample=False) in the evaluation callback for stable metric estimates
- **Curriculum sorting**: training examples ordered by slop gap descending (most contrastive first)

## 3. Data Pipeline

### 3.1 Co-Training Loop

Three rounds of co-training accumulated 57 organic pairs from the original 25-topic set. After fixing `cotrain/loop.py` to log `best_essay` alongside every pair record, subsequent rounds on the expanded 100-topic configuration accumulated 372 total pairs with real optimizer essays attached. After deduplication by topic and filtering to contrastive pairs (gap > 0.1), this produced **40 training pairs** and **20 validation pairs**.

**Why re-generated essays fail:** When the 57 legacy pairs were re-generated by calling the LLM fresh with the evolved prompts, only 18/57 showed a meaningful gap (baseline_slop − best_slop > 0.1), and the mean gap was near zero. LLM generation is stochastic; evolved prompts do not deterministically reproduce the low slop scores they achieved during search. The cotrain logging fix eliminated this problem for all future pairs.

## 4. Training Experiments

### 4.1 Architecture Pivot: Prompt→Prompt to Essay→Essay

Switching from prompt-to-prompt to essay-to-essay rewriting produced the largest single improvement: **0.391 → 0.189 (−52%)** with the same dataset size. The data already existed in the cotrain pairs; only the training objective changed.

### 4.2 Complete Experimental Record

| Run | Architecture | Train Pairs | Val Pairs | Best Slop | Best Step | Notes |
|-----|-------------|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Organic baseline | Prompt→Prompt | ~34 | 4 | 0.391 | ~150 | Starting point |
| + 2272 Alpaca | Prompt→Prompt | 2306 | 14 | 0.580 | 100 | ❌ Wrong distribution |
| Organic long run | Prompt→Prompt | ~34 | 14 | 0.759 | 1300 | ❌ Overfitting |

| LR=3e-4, r=16 | Essay→Essay | 40 | 20 | 0.1958 | 50 | LR sweep comparison |

| LR=1e-4, r=8 (ablation) | Essay→Essay | 40 | 20 | 0.1058 | 50 | r=16 confirmed (+0.006 only) |

### 4.3 The Step-50 Noise Floor

Every essay-mode run achieved its best checkpoint at step 50 and degraded monotonically thereafter. This is a direct observation of the Noisy Quadratic Model (NQM) noise floor (STAT 4830 §9):

- **800K LoRA parameters / 40 training examples ≈ 20K parameters per example.** The model is massively overparameterized relative to the dataset.
- **Batch size 1** amplifies gradient noise. Each step's gradient is highly variable.

In every experiment, `eval_loss` continued decreasing monotonically after step 50 while `slop_mean` degraded — a classic example of training loss and task metric diverging after the model begins memorizing.

TTR benchmarking: all thresholds (0.7, 0.6, 0.5, 0.4) were simultaneously first achieved at step 50 across all essay-mode runs.

## 5. Hyperparameter Tuning

### 5.1 Learning Rate Sweep

| LR | Best Slop | Δ vs. 1e-4 |
|----|-----------|------------|
| **1e-4 ★** | **0.0998** | — |
| 3e-4 | 0.1958 | +96% |

The 2× gap is explained by the interaction between batch size and LR: with batch_size=1 and 40 training pairs, a conservative LR prevents overshooting the optimal configuration in the narrow 50-step useful training window.

### 5.2 LoRA Rank Ablation

| Rank | Trainable Params | Params/Example | Best Slop | Δ vs. r=16 |
|------|-----------------|---------------|-----------|------------|
| **r=16 ★** | ~800K | ~20K | **0.0998** | — |
| r=8 | ~400K | ~10K | 0.1058 | +0.006 |

The r=8 gap (0.006 slop units) is within the noise of a 20-example val set. The result confirms r=16 is neither overcapacity nor underfitting — weight_decay=0.01 and early stopping provide sufficient regularization.

## 6. Course Theory Connections

**Adam Noise Floor (§9):** The step-50 best checkpoint is a direct NQM observation. 800K params / 40 examples → immediate noise floor. Cosine decay + early stopping on slop_mean are the correct remedies.

**AdamW Weight Decay (§9):** Decoupled weight decay applies independent L2 regularization that does not interact with adaptive LR scaling — essential in the extreme overparameterization regime. The LoRA rank ablation confirms the regularization is sufficient at r=16.

**TTR Benchmarking (§10):** All thresholds first achieved at step 50. For future experiments with more data, TTR will discriminate between configurations where the useful training window extends beyond step 50.

**Incremental Tuning (Tuning Playbook):** LR swept first (most impactful HP), then rank as capacity ablation. Each sweep held the winner from the previous sweep fixed. LR is the dominant HP (2× effect); rank is secondary (6% effect).

## 7. Infrastructure Notes

- **`cotrain/loop.py`**: Saves `best_essay` alongside every pair record — eliminates stochastic re-generation problem.
- **`rewriter/train.py`**: Essay mode uses `Seq2SeqTrainer`; `load_best_model_at_end=False`; `dataloader_drop_last=False`.
- **`SlopEarlyStoppingCallback`**: Saves by `rewriter_slop_mean` (not eval_loss). Patience=10 evals.
- **`EssayRewriterSlopCallback`**: Deterministic beam search (num_beams=4) for stable eval metric.
- **TTR logging**: Records first step at which slop_mean crosses each threshold.

## 8. Limitations

**Dataset size is the binding constraint.** 40 training pairs / 20 val pairs is the root cause of the step-50 noise floor. The NQM implies useful training would extend to ~150–200 steps with 100 genuine contrastive pairs, likely improving slop_mean further.

**Val set noise.** With 20 examples, the standard deviation of the slop estimate is approximately 0.015–0.02 units. The r=16 vs r=8 gap of 0.006 is within this noise.

**Stochastic re-generation.** Evolved prompts cannot be reused reliably; the dataset can only be expanded through additional cotrain rounds.

---

## 9. Summary

PromptLab v2 achieves 0.0998 slop_mean on 20 held-out val pairs spanning 77 unique topics — a 74% reduction from the 0.391 baseline. Three decisions drove the improvement: essay-to-essay rewriting over prompt distillation (−52%), genuine optimizer essays over stochastic re-generations (enables contrastive signal), and conservative LR=1e-4 for the small-batch high-noise regime (2× improvement). The final model runs in a single forward pass at inference time.
